# QuantMind user study — information sheet and consent form

## Information sheet

**What this is.** QuantMind is a research prototype of an explainable financial
advisor. It suggests how to split a small investment portfolio across five large
companies and gives a plain-language reason for each suggestion. This study asks a
few people to try the dashboard and tell us how usable and trustworthy they found
it. It is part of a final-year university project.

**What you will do.** In a session of about 15–20 minutes you will: (1) open the
dashboard and create a simple portfolio; (2) read one of its recommendations; (3)
find the reason the system gives for a holding. You will then fill in a short
usability questionnaire and a few questions about your confidence. There are no
right or wrong answers and nothing is being tested about you.

**Risks and benefits.** The study is low-risk: you look at a dashboard and answer a
survey. No real money is involved and nothing you do affects any real account. There
is no direct benefit to you beyond helping improve the tool.

**Your data.** We record only your questionnaire answers, whether you could complete
each task, and short free-text comments. You are identified by a random participant
code (e.g. P03), never by name. Data is stored encrypted, kept until the project is
marked, and then deleted. Results are reported only as group figures and anonymised
quotes. No personal or financial information is collected.

**Voluntary participation.** Taking part is entirely voluntary. You may skip any
question and may stop at any time, without giving a reason and with no consequence;
if you stop, your partial data is discarded on request.

**Ethics.** This study follows the university's ethics process for low-risk studies
with adults. The module ethics quiz was completed with no further action required.
Participants are adults from the researcher's own network; none are under 18,
dependent on the researcher, or in a group needing special protection.

**Contact.** Questions can be directed to the researcher or the module leader.
